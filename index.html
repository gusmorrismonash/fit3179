Melbourne Housing

A Deepdive into the Melbourne Housing Market.

Cheapest Suburb

Melton was the cheapest rated suburb in the time period, showing a mean house price of $510,000, with the lowest priced house in the suburb, 118 Barries Rd costing only $283000. The highest priced house cost $710,000, well below the mean prices of all properties in Melbourne.

Most Expensive

The most expensive suburb to buy in Melbourne was Brighton with a mean property value of $30000000, well above the median property value for Melbourne. The classiest of all being 6 Cole st, valued at $11200000,

On The Rise

Brunswick and the surrounding regions showed to be the biggest risers in property prices over time, showing proportional rises double what other suburbs have experienced. Though property prices have showing no long term depreciation, property prices have shown the most stagnation in the Northern Victoria, region

Yearly Prices

In the line graph depicting real estate trends, a notable dip is observed in mean house and townhouse prices, coinciding with the date, July 2017.
